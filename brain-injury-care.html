Brain Injury Care with Code Blue Care Companion


Code Blue Care Companion is Ready to Save the Day!

The brain, the body's control center, is the most complex organ of the human body. When an injury to the brain occurs, the results can range from minimal to severe damage, depending on the location and level of the injury.

Your loved one living with a traumatic brain injury (TBI) may exhibit changes including behavioral expressions, impaired social and coping skills, and reduced self-esteem. You might have witnessed periods of frustration, anger, agitation, denial, and lack of motivation.

Individuals living with a brain injury may experience cognitive issues affecting their ability to make decisions, plan, and organize. These cognitive impairments can also impact:

  • Short- and long-term memory
  • Concentration and attention spans
  • Completing activities of daily living (ADLs) and instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs)
  • Surgical after-care for people of all ages

Helpful Advice from Code Blue Care Companion

When working with individuals who have a brain injury, it is crucial to understand how the injury affects overall functioning and the impairments that occur due to TBI.

Managing the effects of TBI requires a well-rounded approach, involving multiple disciplines to support care. Various assistive modalities are needed to promote safety, recovery, and a sense of independence.

Adaptive equipment and assistive technology can include devices to help with cognition such as:

  • Sticky labels to identify items in a drawer
  • Calendars, checklists, and schedules
  • Pill organizers for safe medication administration
  • Recording devices for reminders
  • Communication aids

Assistive Devices and Aids to help with movement and strengthening include:

  • Adaptive equipment for eating
  • Sticky liners/surfaces to prevent slippage
  • Appliances for clothing and footwear
  • Adaptive footwear including Velcro-lace shoes or shoes with zippers

Other essential adaptive equipment include:

  • Air pressurized beds to help prevent and manage skin breakdown
  • Aids for bathing and showering
  • Wheelchairs, electric scooters, walkers, and canes

How Code Blue Care Companion Can Help

Caring for someone living with a brain or spinal injury requires patience. For instance, it is important to allow time for a reply to a question or other interaction. Our caregivers are trained in using a multitude of assistive devices to help individuals be as independent as possible. Safety and skill are of utmost importance, along with compassion and reliability.

At Code Blue Care Companion, we provide a wide range of home care support for people living with acute and chronic conditions. Our approach always includes the person and their loved ones in decision-making about their care.


Someone living with TBI will need help with:

Activities of Daily Living (ADLs), including:

  • Mobility to reduce the risk of skin breakdown
  • Bathing
  • Dressing and grooming
  • Providing safe, healthy nutrition and hydration
  • Ensuring safety in the bathroom, especially with toileting assistance

Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADLs), including:

  • Planning, shopping for, and preparing healthy meals
  • Housekeeping and laundry
  • Medication reminders
  • Setting up technology to keep them connected to loved ones
  • Providing safe transportation to appointments and social outings

This is what Code Blue Care Companion does! We not only perform the tasks associated with caring for someone but also empower them through customized social engagement. Contact us today to learn how we can support you and your loved one in managing and overcoming the challenges of living with a brain injury.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is home care?

Home care encompasses a wide range of services that allow people to remain at home as they age, or while they recover from an injury, illness, or medical procedure. It also helps clients develop daily routines to cope with a chronic condition. Home care can include such services as laundry and light housekeeping, meal planning and preparation, transportation, and companionship.

What does in-home care provide?

In-home care assists those who need a hand with activities of daily living, such as dressing, grooming, and meal preparation. In-home care also provides the client’s family with peace of mind, knowing that their loved one has someone looking out for their best interests when family members can’t be there themselves.

What is home health care?

Home health care services place more emphasis on the medical aspects of a client’s home care needs. The home health care services provided by your local Homewatch CareGivers may include medication management, transportation to appointments, and communicating changes in health with family members and medical providers.

Who qualifies for home health care services?

There are many situations when individuals would benefit from home care services. Anyone dealing with a medical condition or injury could qualify for home health care services. Contact your local code blue CareGivers to discuss your home care needs.
